About

The Edo Soft Power Initiative (ESPI)

A Movement focused on how Edo and the Edo Diaspora can harness and optimize our unique, world-renowned and centuries-old creative and knowledge centered cultural heritage; and the adroit deployment of innovative, people and growth-focused mechanisms, to effectively harness the innate soft power dimensions of the former to engender inclusive growth and development for the Edo peoples.

Edo, The Creative Industry, Soft Power and The World

In recent years there has been a growing interest in the role that cultural and creative industries play in developing economies - both in terms of their economic contribution but also in connection with social change and cultural engagement.

Perspective

To reinforce the emerging significance of soft power and to support related policy ' agendas with a particular focus on the role of Non-Central governments and Third Sector Groups, a collaborative initiative such as ESPI is imperative.

ESPI connects and mobilizes different communities, including Development Finance Institutions, impact investors, policy makers and practitioners in the creative/cultural sector, and cultural and creative policy bodies. It seeks to develop a better understanding of the creative economies and its impact on soft power in emerging African City-States such as Edo and explores strategies to encourage and enable sustainable context-specific cultural, social, and economic development.

Strategy

Against the backdrop of the Highlight Notice and the UN 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, this initiative would involve engaging a w range of stakeholders and mobilise resources to accelerate the optimisation of Edo's soft power by leveraging the opportunities presented by its creative industry potentials.

Thus, this initiative will provide a platform for creative practitioners, policy makers, impact investors, and third sector groups to showcase their work. Going further, it will facilitate knowledge exchanges between the Global North and the Global South, highlighting the importance of context-specific knowledge and encouraging connections between local cultural production networks.
